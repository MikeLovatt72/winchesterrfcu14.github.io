Programme Introduction

Welcome to the Summer of Fun

As we look ahead to the U14s season, we've pulled together this resource to help parents and players understand the fundamentals of how they should be looking to train, condition and prepare. This is a partner piece to the Transition to U14s document, where we lay out the key differences between U13s and U14s.

Those differences will, by necessity, drive a more structured approach to how we prepare, coach and execute the U14s season. Essentially the U14s season is a transition year — a deliberate "by design" RFU pathway, bridging the step up from children's rugby to the junior game. The laws, pitch size and team shape all move closer to the adult version of the game — again, by design.

What this means for players

StructurePlayers will start to learn true positional roles and sub-units grow in importance, creating the foundation for the 15-player team.
SpaceMore space to manage and take advantage of while adjusting to the 15-player game.
Set pieceScrums, lineouts and mauls grow in importance and start to be used as attacking weapons and platforms to launch plays.
DimensionsMore options on both sides of the ball — kicking in all aspects of the game, and the addition of flankers.
StrategyUnderstanding the different zones on the pitch and what we are trying to achieve in those zones.

With our progression to an older age group we will look to incorporate approaches and techniques to support player-led goals, player-led consistency, and team developmental goals both on and off the field.

— Martin & the U14 Coaching Team

Our four aims for the season

This programme is built to develop all four key pillars.

1
Game Understanding

Reading the game — shape in attack and defence, knowing where to be, making correct decisions under pressure. What's my next job?

2
Outstanding Behaviours

Attitude, coachability, communication on the pitch, effort, and how a player carries themselves in a team environment.

3
Athletic Potential

Speed, endurance, power, agility, and movement quality. Not just current fitness — potential to develop physically.

4
Fundamental Skills

Catch & Pass, Carry, Tackle, and Breakdown — the four core skills coached and monitored at every session.

Thursday session detail

Format every week: 10 min warm-up · 30 min Skill Zone · 35 min Touch/Tag Game Zone · 15 min cool-down & debrief

19 Jun · Session 1
Catch & Pass — Pillar 1
  • Skill Zone: Passing pairs stationary → moving → game-speed, both hands. 3-man channel at pace. Footwork grid: direction change, cuts, acceleration.
  • Touch/Tag: Must complete 3 passes before touching down. Decision-making under play, not just drills.
  • Key habit: Always lead the receiver. Always follow through.
26 Jun · Session 2
Carry & Evasion — Pillar 2
  • Skill Zone: Gate running — body lean and line-running. 1v0 step, swerve, dummy at pace. Ball security: two-handed carry, never one-handed when tagged.
  • Touch/Tag: Ball carrier must beat one 'touch' before passing. Forces carrying decisions, not just quick offloads.
  • Key habit: Run at defenders. Make them make a decision.
3 Jul · Session 3
Shape in Attack — Game Understanding
  • Skill Zone: 2v1 — carrier commits, passes to support. 3v2 — find the space. Width: stay in lanes, no bunching. Cue: read defender's hips, not their feet.
  • Touch/Tag: Structured phases — must complete 3 phases before scoring. Shape, calls, communication coached throughout.
  • Key habit: Call your name loudly when you want the ball.
10 Jul · Session 4
Defence & Line Speed — Game Understanding
  • Skill Zone: Defensive line — flat, step up on touch, verbal calls. Blitz vs drift: read carrier's hips, communicate the choice. 1v1 jockey footwork.
  • Touch/Tag: Defensive scoring — bonus point for 3 consecutive stops. Creates real defensive intensity and communication habits.
  • Key habit: Set personal communication targets every session.
17 Jul · Session 5
Set Piece Shape (Non-Contact)
  • Skill Zone: Lineout alignment, calling, straight throw (hookers). 8-man scrum formation, CBS sequence, #8 pick-up — movement reps, no contact. Scrum-half decision tree: pass, pick, run, box kick.
  • Touch/Tag: All restarts via lineout free pass or scrum tap. Must exit own half to score.
  • Key habit: Backs 10m back from the lineout. Every time, automatically.
24 Jul · Session 6
Kicking Game — Game Understanding
  • Skill Zone: Punt and box kick (9s & 10s). Kick-chase pairs. Chip/grubber for backs. Kick receipt: full-back positioning under the ball, 'mark' call.
  • Touch/Tag: Kicking permitted. Bonus point for kick-and-chase that prevents a restart.
  • Key habit: Kick with a purpose — territory or pressure, not to get rid of it.
31 Jul · Session 7
Breakdown Technique (Bags/Pads)
  • Skill Zone: Ball presentation on bags — two hands, away from body. Ruck entry: low hips, bind, drive. Jackal: timing, legal body position. All on pads.
  • Touch/Tag: After a touch, carrier presents — support arrives. Defenders respect simulated ruck. Breakdown reads without contact.
  • Key habit: The arriving player always makes the picture better — not worse.
7 Aug · Session 8
Tackle Technique (Pads) + Contact Prep
  • Skill Zone: Tackle footwork on pads — close space, low position, drive through hips. Head to side, below armpits, wrap and drive. From 1 Aug: adapted contact permitted.
  • Touch/Tag: Players call decisions aloud — 'ruck', 'mine', 'yours'. Final non-contact game. Verbal habit-building.
  • Key habit: Technique first, speed second. DPP assessors look at technique above everything.
14 Aug · Session 9
Contact Reintroduction
  • Skill Zone: Tackle technique reintroduced — bag work, then live low-speed contact. Correct technique first, speed second. Head to side, below armpits, wrap and drive.
  • Game Zone: Touch/tag rules for most of game — last 15 mins introduce live tackles. No scrums yet. Lineout free pass restart.
  • RFU Activate warm-up used. Contact technique observed — any welfare concerns addressed immediately.
21 Aug · Session 10
Full Skills Under Pressure
  • Skill Zone: Full DPP skill circuit — Catch & Pass at pace, carry through contact (bags + live), breakdown competition (2v1 ruck), tackle technique.
  • Adapted game: Live tackles throughout. No contested scrums — lineout or tap restart. Full-pitch adapted game. Game Understanding and Behaviours assessed.
  • Closest to a September match. Players identify what they feel confident about.
28 Aug · Session 11
DPP Simulation — Internal Trial
  • Pre-session: team sheet shared. Players briefed on positional roles. Coaches observe — do not direct.
  • Skill Zone (20 mins): individual warm-up with ball. Each player demonstrates Catch & Pass, carrying line, and tackle approach.
  • Adapted trial (40 mins): 2 × 20-min halves. Live tackle, lineout restarts, tap from scrum base. Coaches observe all four DPP pillars.
  • Post-match: Individual feedback. DPP nomination candidates confirmed. Families invited to speak with the coaching team.

RFU Regulation 15 · Appendices 7 & 8 · 2025–26

Moving Up to U14

Everything players and parents need to know about what changes. Based on official RFU Regulation 15, effective 1 August 2025.

The five biggest changes

1

15-a-side

Up from 13-a-side. Two extra players means more space to cover and more positional responsibility for everyone.

2

Full-size pitch

100m × 70m (was 90m × 60m). +11% longer, +17% wider, +50% more ground per player. Fitness matters from day one.

3

8-man scrum

Full 3-4-1 formation. Normal binding now permitted. Number 8 can pick up from the base and run — a major new option.

4

Lineouts begin

U13 has no lineouts — touch restarts with a free pass. U14 introduces proper uncontested lineouts. No lifting until U15.

5

Box kicks & drop goals

The full kicking game opens up. Box kicks, drop goals, and conversions are all introduced at U14 for the first time.

Positional guides

What Changes By Position

At U14 players start to learn true positional roles. Sub-units grow in importance. Here is what changes for each position — and what we will develop together.

Forwards

1 & 3
Props

Now in a full 8-man contested scrum. Front row binding and technique become critical. Outside hips supported by flankers. Hip height relative to shoulder height is a key learning point. Mobility is key — support play and getting to breakdowns are crucial.

2
Hooker

Leads the lineout throw for the first time — must develop a straight, accurate throw. Also hooks in contested scrums. The modern hooker is a hybrid role: highly mobile, a genuine ball-carrying threat across the park.

4 & 5
Locks

Join the full 8-man scrum — normal binding now permitted. Lineout calling and positioning becomes a key role. The beating heart of the pack unit, the engine room behind how the pack operates.

6 & 7
Flankers

Defined breakdown roles in ruck and maul. Lineout positioning. Support work off scrums becomes more structured. Poaching, disrupting, tackling — playing across the field. Works very closely with No.8. At the heart of the pod system.

8
Number 8

Can now pick up from the base of the scrum and run — a major new option that didn't exist at U13. Works closely with flankers to disrupt breakdowns, control defensive lines around the ruck, and ensure the pack unit is performing. Primary ball carrier. Apex predator.

🔑
Forwards key development points: Sub-unit and pack cohesion · Ruck defence and maul defence · Guard/post defensive role · Pod organisation in attack · Set piece platform for starter plays · Mobility across the field.

Backs

9
Scrum-half

Vocal. Very vocal. Needs to be talking all the time — working in close co-operation with his No.8 and No.10. Sets the tempo of the game. Can now box kick from scrum and lineout. Pass, pick, run, or kick. More responsibility at the breakdown. Can be the most influential player on the field.

10
Fly-half

Full portfolio of attacking options now available: kick, pass, carry/run. Running the game in close co-operation with No.9. Box kicks, up-and-unders, cross-field kicks, drop goals all permitted. Dictates use of starter plays from set piece — at the heart of driving shape into the attack.

11 & 14
Wings

Full-size pitch means far more ground to cover. Kick chase, kick return, and counter-attack become key skills. Defensive red zone kicking from hand now an option. Key objective: operate as a back three with the full-back in what is known as a pendulum approach.

12 & 13
Centres

Larger pitch creates more space to exploit. Defensive line speed and depth management become more important. Critical that centres lead defensive lines and tie in with players either side. Line "shooters" is a defensive option. Midfield penetration and support lines on either side of strike runners is a key development point.

15
Full-back

Receiving kicks in a full-size game is a new challenge. Counter-attack from deep, kicking out of hand, covering kicks behind the line. Linking effectively with wingers and covering each other's territory. At the core of the pendulum sub-unit — the Back 3 — and must develop as the last line of defence.

🔑
Backs key development points: Sub-unit and backline cohesion · Shape in attack · Diamond formation · Multi-phase · Defensive line speed as a weapon · Play in the right zones · Kicking as a pressure relief and as a weapon. All backs must be 10m back from lineouts until invited forward by the referee.

Player welfare

Looking After Yourself

It is critical that players start to understand the role they play in their own welfare. These are not suggestions — they are the non-negotiables of being a serious rugby player.

Warming Up — Non-Negotiable

Cold muscles tear. Warm muscles bend. A 10-minute warm-up raises muscle temperature, increases blood flow, and prepares tendons and ligaments for explosive loads. At U14, your body is still developing — skipping the warm-up is the single biggest cause of avoidable injuries in junior rugby.

A proper warm-up also improves reaction time, sprint speed, and passing accuracy from the first minute of a session. Players who warm up properly perform better immediately — not just later.

What good looks like (5–10 mins, every session):
  • Leg swings, arm circles, hip rotations
  • Walking knee drives, lateral shuffles
  • A-skips, bounding, change of direction
  • Gentle jog building to strides
  • Thursday sessions use RFU Activate from Phase 3

Recovery — Where Adaptation Happens

Training breaks the body down. Recovery builds it back stronger. Muscles grow, aerobic fitness improves, and neural pathways sharpen during rest — not during the session. A warm-down signals the body to switch from "performance" mode to "repair" mode.

Warm-down routine (5 mins after every session):
  • Easy jog or walk — 2 minutes
  • Hip flexor stretch — 30s each side
  • Quad & hamstring stretch — 30s each
  • Calf & thoracic rotation — 30s each

Sleep

8–9 hours minimum per night. Growth hormone — responsible for muscle repair and development — is released primarily during deep sleep. No sleep shortcut exists. Sleep deficits cannot be recovered. This is the most powerful recovery tool available to a U14 player.

Aim for 8–9 hours. Every night. Non-negotiable.

Nutrition & Hydration

The body needs clean lean protein, complex carbs, fibre and healthy fats to support growth and recovery. Eat within 60 minutes of finishing a session — carbohydrates to restore energy, protein to repair muscle.

Hydration: if you feel thirsty, it's already too late. Monitor urine colour. Getting your hydration levels right takes time and attention — everyone has their own levels and you need to learn yours.

Compound weights not needed yet. Stretching, flexibility and mobilisation are preferable.

Pain vs Effort

There is a difference between the discomfort of hard effort — lungs burning, legs heavy — and actual pain. If you feel sharp or persistent pain, stop immediately. Tell a parent and your coach.

Never train on consecutive days if carrying an injury. A week off now beats missing pre-season. Rehabbing an injury correctly is far more important than training through it.

Limit contact sessions to once within any 48-hour period.

For Parents

  • Half-game rule is mandatory — every player must play at least half of available time. Speak to the coach if this isn't happening.
  • No lifting at U14 — lineout lifting is only permitted from U15. Mandatory safety rule.
  • Scrums are closely managed — the 1.5m push limit is strictly enforced.
  • Match ends at 50-point difference — RFU rules protect players in mismatched fixtures.
  • Medical info — please share any medical condition, injury history or welfare concern with the coaching team before the season starts.
